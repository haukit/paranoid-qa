**Aviation Incident Report**

**Registration:** N123AB  
**Date:** March 15, 2023  
**Location:** Greenfield Municipal Airport (GFM), Greenfield, USA  

**Sequence of Events:**  
At approximately 10:30 AM, a single-engine flight trainer, a Cessna 172, took off from runway 27 at Greenfield Municipal Airport. The aircraft, piloted by a student with an instructor onboard, climbed to an altitude of 800 feet when the engine suddenly lost power. The instructor immediately took control, attempting to troubleshoot the situation while maintaining a stable descent.

The aircraft's descent rate increased, prompting the pilot to declare an emergency to the control tower. The instructor identified an open field approximately 1 mile west of the airport and aimed for a controlled landing. The aircraft touched down in the field, sustaining minor damage to the landing gear but avoiding any injuries to the occupants.

Emergency services were dispatched and arrived on the scene shortly after the landing to ensure the safety of the pilots. The aircraft was subsequently examined for mechanical failure.

**Findings:**  
Investigators discovered that a fuel line had become disconnected from the carburetor, leading to an immediate loss of fuel supply to the engine. The maintenance records indicated that a recent service had been performed on the fuel system, but the connection was not properly secured.

**Probable Cause:**  
Improper reassembly of the fuel system during maintenance led to a loss of engine power shortly after takeoff.
