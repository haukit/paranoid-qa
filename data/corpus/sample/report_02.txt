**Incident Report: TWN-0423**

**Date:** March 15, 2023  
**Location:** 15 miles southeast of Darnell Regional Airport (DRN), Montana  
**Registration:** N123AB  
**Aircraft:** Beechcraft Baron 58  

**Sequence of Events:**  
On March 15, 2023, at approximately 14:30 UTC, a twin-engine Beechcraft Baron 58 operating as charter flight TWN-0423 was en route from Helena to Darnell Regional Airport. The flight was cruising at an altitude of 12,000 feet when the crew reported encountering severe icing conditions. The flight had previously received weather updates indicating possible light icing; however, the conditions worsened unexpectedly. 

The pilot attempted to climb to a higher altitude to escape the icing but experienced a significant loss of control as the aircraft became increasingly heavy with ice accumulation. The crew declared an emergency and initiated a descent to 8,000 feet in an effort to stabilize the aircraft. During the descent, the pilots struggled to maintain control, and the aircraft began to yaw and roll. 

After a tense few minutes, the pilots regained control and successfully diverted to Darnell Regional Airport, executing a rough but safe landing at 14:55 UTC. No injuries were reported among the six passengers and two crew members. 

**Findings:**  
Post-incident inspection revealed extensive ice accumulation on the wings and control surfaces. The aircraft's de-icing systems were inspected and found to be functional. Weather reports indicated that conditions were rapidly changing, contributing to the severity of the icing encountered.

**Probable Cause:**  
Inadequate pre-flight weather assessment led to an unexpected encounter with severe icing conditions.
